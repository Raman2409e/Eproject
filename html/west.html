MORE INFO ABOUT MONUMENTS

Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower, located in Paris, France, was completed in 1889 as the centerpiece for the 1889 World's Fair. Designed by engineer Gustave Eiffel, it was initially criticized but has since become one of the most recognizable landmarks in the world. Standing 1,083 feet tall, the tower was the tallest man-made structure on Earth until the completion of the Chrysler Building in 1930. It’s a symbol of France’s modernity and is visited by millions of people each year.

Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty, located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, was a gift from France to the United States in 1886. Designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi, it symbolizes freedom, democracy, and hope. Standing at 305 feet tall, it depicts a woman holding a torch in one hand and a tablet inscribed with the date of the U.S. Declaration of Independence in the other. The statue remains an iconic symbol for immigrants and is one of the most recognized landmarks in the world.

Colosseum

The Colosseum, located in Rome, Italy, is an ancient amphitheater that was completed in 80 AD. It could hold up to 50,000 spectators and was primarily used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and animal hunts. Known for its impressive Roman engineering, the Colosseum is an iconic symbol of ancient Rome’s power and culture. It remains one of the most visited and recognizable historical monuments in the world today.

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is an ancient Incan city located high in the Andes Mountains of Peru. Built in the 15th century, it was likely a royal estate or religious site. The site is known for its well-preserved ruins, stunning terraces, and breathtaking views. Rediscovered in 1911 by Hiram Bingham, it is now one of the most visited archaeological sites in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage site. Its precise purpose and construction methods remain a subject of fascination.

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer is a massive statue of Jesus Christ located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Standing 98 feet tall, with arms stretched wide, it overlooks the city from the top of Mount Corcovado. Completed in 1931, the statue is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone, symbolizing Christianity and serving as a symbol of peace and unity. It is one of the New Seven Wonders of the World and a major tourist attraction.

Big Ben

Big Ben is the nickname for the Great Bell of the Elizabeth Tower at the Palace of Westminster in London, England. Completed in 1859, the tower is 316 feet tall, and the bell itself weighs over 13 tons. The clock faces are an iconic symbol of London, and the tower is often mistakenly referred to as Big Ben itself. The structure is a major landmark and tourist attraction, representing British heritage and history.
